In recent years, policies have been implemented at all levels of government to help realise the potential of using cycling (Pucher & Buehler 2012) to improve the overall sustainability of our transport system and the liveability of our cities. Since the launch of the original OneNYC strategy in 2015, New York City has been securing a sustainable future by mentioning its commitment to improving AIR QUALITY: for example, promoting CLEAN ENERGY vehicles, increasing GREEN SPACES facilities, and encouraging travel by BICYCLE and using the public transit system.

This site will aim to explore whether New York City's air quality has improved even further over the past decade to the present (2011-2021) and to show how improvements in air quality relate to clean energy, green space amenities and bicycle use.
